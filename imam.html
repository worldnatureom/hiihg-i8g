Imam Ali ibn Abi Talib (Arabic: عَلِيُّ بْن أَبِي طَالِب, c. 600–661 CE) was the cousin and son-in-law of the Prophetﷺ, and was the fourth Rashidun caliph who ruled from 656 CE to 661, as well as the first. Born to Abu Talib ibn Abd al-Muttalib and Fatima bint Asad, young Imam Ali was raised by his elder cousin, which is the Prophetﷺ himself and was among the first to accept Islam.

Imam Ali is revered for his courage, honesty, unbending devotion to Islam, magnanimity, and equal treatment of all Muslims. For his admirers, he has thus become the archetype of uncorrupted Islam and pre-Islamic chivalry. Ahlus Sunnah regard him as the last of the rashidun caliphs, while the Rawafidh take him as the first caliph rejecting the Caliphate of Abu Bakr, Umar and Uthman, May Allah be pleased with them all. May Allah either guide, or curse the Rawafidh.

The Prophet ﷺ said that no one but a believer would love Ali, and none but a hypocrite would nurse grudge against Ali.

Saheeh Al Muslim 78

This proves that whoever loves Imam Ali is a Mu'min with complete faith. While those who hate Imam Ali are none but Munafiqeen, they might even fall to Kufr for their enmity against Imam Haider e Karrar

Rasoolallah ﷺ said that "For whomsoever I am a Maula, Ali is his Maula. Oh Allah, love the one who loves Ali, and hate the one who hates Ali!"

Al Bidaya Wan Nihaya 18/668

This indicates the significance of Imam Ali in the Ummah of Rasoolallah ﷺ and this also indicates that indeed Imam Ali is the one loved by Allah and his Wali, same as Shaykhain Abu Bakr and Umar.
